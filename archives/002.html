Challenge: Design a two‑impulse transfer from a circular parking orbit (800 km, i = 63.4°) to a Tundra orbit with a 24‑hour period (86164.1 s) and eccentricity 0.3. The final orbit must have argument of perigee fixed at 270° to maximize dwell time over the northern hemisphere, with apogee stationed above 37.48°S, 135.23°E. Use GMAT's differential corrector to achieve both targets simultaneously.

The first burn establishes the transfer ellipse; the second burn circularizes the apogee while achieving the required period and eccentricity. Both burns are optimized via a two‑target corrector sequence.

1. Transfer Burn

First impulse at initial perigee raises apogee to establish transfer ellipse. Targets: post‑burn orbit period ≈ 86164.1 s with intermediate eccentricity.

2. Coast to Apogee

Propagate along transfer ellipse to apogee of the final orbit. Duration determined by transfer orbit period.

3. Circularization Burn

Second impulse at apogee adjusts period to exactly 86164.1 s and eccentricity to 0.3. Combined maneuver ensures final AoP = 270°.

Detailed Analysis

Transfer Orbit Parameters

  • Initial SMA (800 km circ): a₁ = 7178.0 km
  • Target SMA (Tundra): a₂ = 42164.0 km (GEO radius)
  • Transfer SMA: a_trans = (a₁ + a₂)/2 = 24671.0 km
  • Transfer ΔV₁ = √(μ(2/a₁ − 1/a_trans)) − √(μ/a₁) ≈ 2.42 km/s

Final Orbit Parameters

  • SMA: a = 42164.0 km
  • ECC: e = 0.3
  • r_perigee = a(1−e) = 29514.8 km (≈ 23136 km altitude)
  • r_apogee = a(1+e) = 54813.2 km (≈ 48435 km altitude)
  • Period: T = 2π√(a³/μ) = 86164.1 s ✓
