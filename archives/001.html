Challenge: A satellite is inserted into a 300 km circular orbit (i = 98.8°, RAAN = 306°, AoP = 0°, TA = 54°). The objective is to plan maneuvers to reach an 800 km Sun‑synchronous orbit (i = 98.558°) such that the satellite passes over a randomly chosen ground location 19.72°N, 82.98°E at approximately the same local time each day (target LTAN = 10:00).

Two initial maneuvers were executed: a Hohmann transfer to raise the orbit to 800 km, followed by an inclination correction burn at the new apogee. After raising, the average local time of passes over the target was 9:53. This 7‑minute offset (1.75° in RAAN) was corrected by exploiting the faster nodal precession at 300 km. An appropriate coasting period was inserted before the Hohmann transfer so that the RAAN drifts enough to achieve the desired 10:00 LTAN.

1. Pre‑Drift Coast

Coast at 300 km for 5.68 days, allowing RAAN to precess eastward at 1.29°/day (vs. 0.982°/day after raising). Accumulated ΔΩ = 1.75°.

2. Hohmann Transfer

Two burns: first at perigee (300 km → 800 km apogee), second at new apogee to circularize. ΔV ≈ 0.56 km/s.

3. Inclination Adjustment

Combined with second Hohmann burn at apogee to correct Δi = 0.242°. ΔV ≈ 0.03 km/s.

Detailed Analysis

Nodal Precession Rates

  • At 300 km: Ω̇₃₀₀ = +1.29°/day
  • At 800 km: Ω̇₈₀₀ = +0.982°/day
  • Difference: ΔΩ̇ = 0.308°/day

RAAN Correction

  • LTAN offset: 9:53 → 10:00 = 7 min
  • Equivalent RAAN shift: 7 min × 0.25°/min = 1.75°
  • Coast time at 300 km: T = 1.75° / 0.308°/day ≈ 5.68 days

Maneuver Sequence Timing

The satellite first coasts in the initial 300 km orbit for 5.68 days, allowing RAAN to drift eastward by 1.75° relative to the no‑coast case. Then the Hohmann transfer is initiated, raising the orbit to 800 km. At the new apogee, the inclination burn is performed (combined with circularization). Post‑maneuver, the ground track repeats with LTAN = 10:00 over the target (19.72°N, 82.98°E).
